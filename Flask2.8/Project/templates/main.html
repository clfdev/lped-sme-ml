This app uses a machine learning model (Regularized Gradient Boosting) to obtain the local LPED and SME of a molecular complex.

LPED stands for Local potential energy density and it is obtained from QTAIM data.

SME stands for supramolecular energy and it is the total energy of interaction in a molecular complex.

A (molecular) complex is the union of two (or more) substances (molecules) held together by intermolecular interactions.

In case there is just one intermolecular interaction, the local SME becomes the global SME.

In order to obtain the global SME for a complex with multiple interactions:

  1. Firstly obtain all local SME one by one
  2. Finally sum them up

Or one can use the Group prediction option (see below or at the right).

Beforehand, the user needs:

  1. A quantum chemistry package to generate the optimized geometry of the molecular complex.
  2. Choose Merz-Singh-Kollman (MK), ChelpG or RESP scheme to obtain the corresponding atomic charges.
  3. It is recommended to use a quantum method that takes into account the dispersion correction during the optimization process.

For the prediction analysis, the user needs to:

  1. Inform the MK (or ChelpG or RESP) atomic charges of the two atoms involved in the interatomic interaction.
  2. Inform the interatomic distance between the same interacting atoms whose atomic charges were given.

The prediction depends upon the quality of theoretical level for the optimization geometry.

The prediction also depends on the quality of the atomic charge scheme used.

Important:the dataset was designed to place the atomic negative charge in "Atomic charge A".
One must remember this when inputing atomic charges if there are positive and negative charges involved.

The dataset for the machine learning modeling was obtained from our previous works on LPED plus new data.

Metrics: R2=0.861, MAE = 1.06 kcal mol-1 Bohr-3, MSE = 1.43 kcal2 mol-2 Bohr-6, and RMSE = 1.19 kcal mol-1 Bohr-3
